# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary**: Parents of K-12 students in Western Sydney (particularly Canley Heights / Fairfield area) who are actively evaluating tutoring options. They arrive with high standards and some scepticism — they want proof of results, not just promises. Decision timeline is days to weeks; the key action is booking a consultation.

**Secondary**: Students (Year 3–12) who will use the site to understand what DA offers and feel welcomed. They need to feel inspired, not pressured.

**Tertiary**: Prospective teachers exploring whether DA is worth their time.

## Product Purpose

DA Tuition is a premium, small-group K-12 tutoring service in Canley Heights, NSW, operating since 2005. The website's job is to earn parental trust and convert it into a booked consultation. Success looks like a parent thinking: "This is clearly the best option around — I want to book now."

The site also sustains confidence in existing families and builds long-term brand equity for word-of-mouth referral.

## Brand Personality

Inspiring · Grounded · Proven

- **Inspiring**: Students and parents leave feeling that real improvement is possible and within reach.
- **Grounded**: 20+ years of real results, real families, no hype — confidence comes from evidence.
- **Proven**: Award-winning, 650+ students helped, testimonials from real families, HSC outcomes that speak for themselves.

Voice: warm authority. Like a trusted expert friend who happens to be the best in their field. Not formal, not salesy, never condescending.

## Anti-references

- **Cheap tutoring chains**: No cluttered layouts, discount-style badges, garish CTAs, or bulk-enrolment messaging. DA is boutique; it should feel like it.
- **Generic school websites**: No stiff institutional grids, government-palette greys, or jargon-heavy copy that reads like a syllabus.
- **Cold SaaS / tech startup**: No all-white minimalism, floating sans-serif everywhere, or product-UI aesthetics. This is human, warm, and place-based — not an app.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence over assertion** — Show results, real quotes, real names, real awards. Let proof do the persuading.
2. **Warmth earns trust** — Every section should feel like it was made by people who genuinely care, not a marketing team. Colour, imagery, and copy all contribute.
3. **Premium by restraint** — Elevated design comes from what you leave out. White space, type hierarchy, and calm colour transitions signal quality better than visual noise.
4. **Scroll should feel like a journey** — The gradient transition system and animation pacing should reward curiosity. Each section reveals something new without feeling like a brochure.
5. **Never talk down to parents** — They are smart, researching adults making a significant investment in their child. Copy and UX should treat them accordingly.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal compliance target specified. Aim for WCAG 2.1 AA contrast ratios on all text, semantic HTML throughout, and keyboard-accessible interactive components as a baseline. Animations should degrade gracefully for users with reduced-motion preferences where feasible.
