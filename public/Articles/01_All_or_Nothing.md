THE ‘All or Nothing’ MENTALITY

“All the best art comes from colouring outside the lines”

STUCK…

Mistake, after mistake, after mistake. You pick yourself up again, and again. But still, you fall once more.

It becomes this cycle that has only one end in sight. In big, bolded letters at the very front of your mind, it shouts ‘GIVE UP’. And so, like an easy escape, you do. You give up. You are only left to wonder what you have learnt or what you will do next. You feel empty, don’t you?

What if I told you that every time you stumble, just like the time that you received 60% for your exam that you studied so hard for, or that in-class quiz that you barely managed to pass, or the essay that cannot stick in your head no matter what, is really one step closer to your best self?

It is so easy to think ‘If I can’t do this, then I can’t do anything’. So you’re eventually trapped in the ‘All or Nothing’ mentality. However, the mistakes that you make as both a student and a person are not indicators of failure but rather, learning opportunities paving your way to growth.

MEET NOVA

My friend, Nova, loves to colour. In fact, she considers herself a professional!

However, one thing about Nova is that she follows a strict technique to avoid colouring out the lines. Nova always perfectly outlines her drawings first then colours in the gaps. Outline. Colour. Outline. Colour. Outline. Col–

Her hand slips. A bright red streak is left slashing her paper as her mind tells her:

‘My image is ruined’. ‘I should never colour again’. ‘I am the worst artist in the world’.

These are the same exact thoughts that run through my head and most likely yours when we can’t seem to see something right. From there, we just want to give up.

But Nova realised that restarting her whole work instead of embracing the tiny flaws might have been ignoring the way to find a quick solution to her issue.

Instead, with a few strokes, the red streak became the feature of her artwork. It was special!
