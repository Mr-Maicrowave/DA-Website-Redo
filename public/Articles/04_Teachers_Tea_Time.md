Writing with Gru-titude: How Mr Danny leads DA’s Minions

Spotted! DA’s very own Gru, a.k.a. Mr Danny, turning his classroom into creative magic one sneeze at a time.
From his booming laughs echoing through our pastel classrooms to pulling off an epic Halloween costume, Mr Danny is no ordinary tutor. But how did this English-hating rebel transform into DA’s writing hero?

Ready for a little inside scoop?

Mr Danny has been lighting the way for young minds to sharpen their most dreaded English skill – writing.

With an unmatched fire in his words, he has endeavoured to change kids’ mindsets, their attitudes, them. One baby step at a time.

I asked Danny one day why he loved working at DA. He didn’t just spill the tea. He poured the whole POT. His answer was one of those rare moments of vulnerability that hit me:

“I want to have a big impact on kids who feel like people have given up on them, who have discarded them and dismissed them, subtly or directly suggesting they could not amount to anything. I work better with these kids because I personally know what it feels like to have people not believing in me.”

If you squinted, you might’ve seen his eyes glisten for a second.

However, did you know Mr Danny wasn’t always the person we see today? Let’s rewind to his youth – a lost adolescent, swept up in frivolous mischief and drifting away from the HSC finish line.

I mean, he is human, like the rest of us. Contrary to popular opinion, Mr Danny is not as ‘old’ as you think. He grew up in the age of phones and computer games, making their lure hardest to resist.

So young Danny had lost his way, getting into his fair share of trouble, finding himself distracted by more instant gratifying ‘fun’.

Cue: Enter Miss Amanda, the fairy godmother of academic transformations, who sat him down for a nice chat. Her ultimatum was simple: continue drifting or turn your life around.

What transpired was nothing short of amazing.

For Mr Danny, this was the wake-up call he never knew he needed, a bucket of water poured onto him, drenching him out of complacency and nonchalance.

Choosing redemption, he sprang, cleaned his life. To reclaim himself, he swept away the clutter – phone, games, social media – that were blocking him from his success. With self-discipline, he ditched those 3:00am DOTA battles for study sessions and sent “Sorry, can’t” texts to weekend plans.

Throughout Years 11 and 12, he buckled down and drowned out the noise. The noise of his former life. Lying on the cake? His life change inspired his friends to also knuckle down.

You must be thinking… This seems impossible. Did he really do this?

YES!

Of course, no transformation comes without a plot twist. With such a sacrifice, depriving himself of his desires to ‘just relax’, surely, he would have run out of fuel. He certainly did, in his third HSC assessment.

His mark plummeted in one task and he battled his inner saboteurs, quick to coerce him to just throw in the towel. They told him it was too late, that there was nothing he could do. They told him to surrender because all of this was not worth the suffering. They told him to feed onto his self-pity.

The battle was fierce, but he refused to back down.

“So what kept you going?” I asked.

“My tutors. Then, my friends.”

Regaining his composure, he staged the ultimate comeback, smashing his trial exams jumping from 29th to 3rd place. POWER MOVE!

Learning isn’t a straight line. It’s a wave with inevitable pitfalls. Alongside our teachers, tutors and parents, failure is the one teacher we often ignore. Painfully cruel, yet indisputably fair.

This ignorance has fuelled our hatred and fear of failure. Instead, we must embrace failure, no matter how much it pricks our egos and bruises our pride.

As long as you’re moving in the right direction, you’re closer to the goal. What kept Mr Danny floating on this wave? He said that every little action adds up.

Ignoring that alluring phone notification.

Resisting the ‘start’ button on a game.

Sacrificing ‘precious’ weekends to study at tutor.

What a beautiful paradox. Mr Danny has always hated English.

Honestly though, who hasn’t? His dislike drove him to discover new ways to make English tolerable. Through Mr Danny’s passionate teaching style, coupled with the right dose of some tough love, he has polished some diamonds in the rough in his little DA minions.

Recently, most of his selective students received offers from their selected schools, many ranking in the top 10% in writing statewide. Not too shabby, good sir! One of the parents could not believe her eyes that her son, who once could not string together coherent sentences and whose writing failed to be engaging, had morphed into a polished and proficient writer.

The best part? Many students now love English “because of Mr Danny.”

For someone who is a hard nut to crack, Mr Danny is all but a big softie at heart. As DA’s very own lovable Gru – a walking contradiction of toughness and warmth, he has transformed into the most caring of tutors, one who never lets his minions down. Beneath his gruff exterior is a fiery dedication cementing him as one of our favs!

These small choices are your investments. In the short term, you see little to no reward. You even see the losses. You are tempted to withdraw your investment. But you stay patient. You stay consistent. You have faith and trust. These pay dividends.

Mr Danny never regretted this choice. This is why every student needs that one mentor who sees their potential, even when they can’t see it themselves. Someone who has been in their shoes. Mr Danny, the self-confessed “English-hater!” knows this struggle all too well.

And that’s the inside scoop on DA’s own Gru!
Until next time, remember: every hero has their own origin story. Keep your pencils sharp and your minds sharper.
