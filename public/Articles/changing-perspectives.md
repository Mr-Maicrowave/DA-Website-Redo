How Changing Perspectives Unlocks Your Potential

Let me tell you a story…

I once knew a girl who used to love maths. She was so talented that she would challenge students one year older without hesitation and WIN. Every week Miss Amanda paired her with the stiffest competition in class and she would WIN. For homework, she often came FIRST.

But somewhere along the way she gave up. Stopped believing in herself. Began to despise math. This same girl would come to fail her first Year 12 Math Advanced exam (scoring a whopping 46%).

This girl was ME.

Like you, I have experienced failure. We all have at some point, just in different ways. But we shouldn’t be afraid to fail, we should only be afraid of not getting back up.

I remember saying, “The exam was unfair!” “We never learned half the concepts in that test!” In fact… we did. But I blamed the world, the exam, and my school teachers. Everyone… except myself.

I was playing the blame game. I’m sure we have all been a player in this toxic game at one point, or perhaps you are still playing. I locked myself in my room for days telling myself “I can’t do math…” but I gave myself an ultimatum that day.

Instead, I told myself “I can’t do math YET” and decided to drop to Standard Math. 3 months from my HSC, grind 1.5 years’ worth of content in the 2 week holiday, and score 98. So who says YOU can’t do the same?

Remember that no one is perfect not even the tutors who seem to have all the answers. We have all made mistakes, we are only human after all, but we didn’t make it this far because we decided to give up, throw in the towel, and blame the world for our mistakes. We all have a shared goal of inspiring the future generation, and passing on our knowledge and wisdom, and to do that we had to be defeated, respawn, level up, and come back to challenge the same boss that defeated us.

The 180-Degree Shift
So let me challenge you:

What if instead of saying “I can’t do this”, you said, “I can’t do this YET”?

What if instead of saying “I’m not good at this”, you said, “I’m not good at this YET”?

What if instead of saying “I don’t understand this”, you said, “I don’t understand this YET”?

Don’t be fooled by this simple three-letter word. It has the power to change the way we view challenges and failure. Trust me when I say the 6 years you spend in high school go by in the blink of an eye, sometimes it feels like the sun sets when we haven’t achieved anything in the day. Time does not wait and yet we do.

We wait for our dreams to magically come true just like in the fairytales.
We wait for opportunities to come to us.
We wait for that perfect moment to try, to feel ready, to feel confident.

The truth is that if we keep waiting, that moment may never come. So why spend your precious time dabbling in the words “I can’t” and “I don’t”?

The power of YET reminds us that we don’t have everything all figured out, and that’s okay! Let it be a reminder that we just have to start. Progress is made, not by waiting, but by showing up, putting in the effort, and believing that growth will come.

If you have made it this far, it means you pledge to embrace the power of the word “YET” and in the wise words of Gen Alpha:

If you give up, YOU’RE COOKED!!!
