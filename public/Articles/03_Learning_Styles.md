FIND OUT MORE ABOUT YOUR LEARNING STYLE
AUDITORY LEARNER

An auditory learner is someone who learns best through hearing and listening. These are the kids who seem to remember what you say more than what you show or write down.

TIPS:

Ask lots of questions to your DA tutor in class

Explain your study notes to your friends, siblings and even your pet dog!

Try out a DA CLASS (not private) for a lively and engaging atmosphere

It’s amazing how much they can absorb when the material is delivered in a way that speaks to their strengths—literally!

VISUAL LEARNER

A visual learner is someone who processes information best through sight. If you’ve ever noticed a child who loves doodling, colour-coding, or saying, “Can you show me?” they might just be a visual learner.

TIPS:

Make colourful flashcards with your stationery kit hiding in your cupboard

Highlight key points or organise information with different colours. Try colour-coding notes—it’s like giving your brain a visual roadmap.

Teaching visual learners is all about helping them “see” the lesson come to life. It’s like giving their minds the puzzle pieces they need to solve the whole picture!

KINAESTHETIC LEARNER

A kinaesthetic learner is someone who learns best by doing. If you’ve ever noticed a child who can’t sit still for long, prefers building or creating things, they might be a kinaesthetic learner.

TIPS:

Write, draw, or physically create something related to the lesson. Building models, crafting projects, or even just doodling while they learn can help anchor the content.

Breaks are compulsory! (Don’t be an Academic Burnout, be an Academic Weapon!)

When you’re actively engaged, you don’t just learn—you feel the lesson come alive!!
