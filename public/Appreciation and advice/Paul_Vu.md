# Paul Vu

## Appreciation

The reason for my fantastic track record comes down to two reasons, choosing a subject I care about and having a drive to force me to work consistently. Focusing on areas I am genuinely interested in made studying much more enjoyable and less taxing. Even though I am a very math-centred person who thinks in a more logical manner, I still chose to pursue music and still managed to come first, something that never could have happened if I did not enjoy this subject. Of course, passion was not enough to prop me ahead of everyone else, so the effort had to be placed, which came from the many sources of motivation around me. I had both teachers and tutors pushing me to do the best I could and not waste my ability as well as the need to succeed for my family’s financial situation. It was a combination of both these factors that drove me into first for so many of my exams.

## Advice for current students

I cannot stress enough the significance of collaboration in helping both you and others in succeeding. In cases where both students are of equal ability, the two perspectives allow for the identification and rectification of gaps in understanding. In mixed-ability groups, however, what may appear on the surface as one person pestering another actually benefits both parties. For the learner, seeking clarification helps them understand concepts more deeply. For the educator, the process of teaching requires them to solidify their understanding to explain effectively. Additionally, the questions given by learner are often more difficult in nature, providing a challenge the educator and thus enhancing their skills further.
