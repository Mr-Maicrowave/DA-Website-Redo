# Ryan Than

## Appreciation

If you think every student who tops their grade was born brilliant, think again. Some people are blessed with natural talent but that wasn’t the case for me.  

When I entered high school, I was failing or barely passing every English assessment. The subject felt like an insurmountable wall. Unlike maths, where my parents could step in to help, I felt completely on my own. But everything changed when I joined DA and heard something that shifted my perspective: It’s not my parents’ job to do my assignments. My poor marks weren’t due to a lack of help, they came from gaps in my skills and foundation.  

A turning point came one evening at tutoring when I sat next to a girl in maths who shared her story. She had once struggled with English as I did, yet now she was consistently topping the grade after joining Ms Lai’s class. That conversation sparked hope in me, if she could do it, why couldn’t I? Belief is powerful, but belief combined with the right effort and support changes everything.  

With Ms Lai’s guidance, I learned that a great tutor does more than teach content; they instil discipline, correct your weaknesses, and push you to exceed your own expectations. She taught me to commit to the process, I worked harder, and did what was necessary rather than what was comfortable and easy. I took on double English to make up for lost time, and now, while I don’t love the subject, I do enjoy it and I consistently score A range marks. Definitely a far cry from my ‘barely passing’ days.  

It turns out success isn’t reserved for the “naturally gifted”. It’s for those willing to embrace the right mindset, seek the right guidance, and put in the work.

## Advice for current students

My advice to any student at DA is simple: never accept the idea that you’re just “not good” at something. Skills aren’t set in stone. They can be developed. I know this firsthand. I started in the bottom English class, and now I’m not only in the top class but consistently achieving top 3. It didn’t happen overnight, and it certainly wasn’t easy. But if you keep trying, you will see progress. Even if you don’t reach the very top, you’ll climb higher than you thought possible. Giving up, on the other hand, guarantees that nothing will change.  

One of the most important lessons I’ve learned is to seek the right help and actually use it. Don’t hesitate to ask your parents or teachers for guidance because believe it or not, they actually want to see you succeed. For me, changing tutors was a turning point. Receiving the right support was life-changing: my marks improved, my confidence soared, and I genuinely began to enjoy English (something I never thought possible).  

That success has inspired me to take the same approach with maths next term. I’m ready to put in the work, seek help, and build my skills. If English has taught me anything, it’s this: the right mindset, combined with the right support, can turn any weakness into a strength. Don’t be afraid to start, don’t be afraid to struggle. Just don’t stop.
